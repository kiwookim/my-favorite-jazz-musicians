Jazz Greats : Three Joes

Joe Henderson

Born: April 24, 1937, Lima, OH Died: June 30, 2001, San Francisco, CA

Joe Henderson was an American jazz tenor saxophonist. In a career spanning more than four decades, Henderson played with many of the leading American players of his day and recorded for several prominent labels, including Blue Note, Milestone, and Verve

Joe Lovano

Born: December 29, 1952 (age 69 years), Cleveland, OH

Joseph Salvatore Lovano is an American jazz saxophonist, alto clarinetist, flautist, and drummer. He has earned a Grammy Award and several mentions on Down Beat magazine's critics' and readers' polls. His wife, with whom he records and performs, is singer Judi Silvano

Joe Pass

Born: January 13, 1929, New Brunswick, NJ Died: May 23, 1994, Los Angeles, CA

Joe Pass was an American jazz guitarist. Pass is well known for his work stemming from numerous collaborations with pianist Oscar Peterson and vocalist Ella Fitzgerald, and is often heralded as one of the most unique and notable jazz guitarists of the 20th century
